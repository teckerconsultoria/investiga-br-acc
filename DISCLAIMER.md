# DISCLAIMER — Signals, Not Proof

Policy-Version: v1.0.0  
Effective-Date: 2026-02-28  
Owner: WTG Governance Team

## Patterns are signals, not proof

Graph patterns indicate relationships in public data.  
They do not by themselves prove illegality, intent, or guilt.

WTG output is not legal advice and is not a substitute for formal legal or investigative process.

## Source quality limitations and false-positive caveat

Public datasets may contain:

- Delays and incomplete periods.
- Source-level schema changes.
- Identifier ambiguity or masked fields.
- Documentation gaps and extraction errors.

All high-impact conclusions require independent corroboration.

## Temporal and identity confidence caveat

Interpretation must account for:

- Temporal ordering (event before consequence).
- Identity quality tier (strong vs probable).
- Coverage windows and missing-month caveats.

When uncertainty exists, outputs must be framed as hypotheses requiring further verification.
